# Zero to Hero — Data Science Path

> Personal learning journal — tracking my path from scratch to deep expertise in data science.

## Introduction

AI is growing faster and faster. Every day, new models with better performance and better benchmarks. I can see that its impact on jobs that were untouchable before — like developers, designers, writers — is becoming more and more evident.

Since I'm a data scientist, I believe that junior engineers will suffer in their early careers. We'll see if the junior market will get wiped out or not. My take is: as a developer (SWE, DS, MLE...), if you only use your brain on the surface level, you won't be needed anymore, as AI can do it faster, cheaper, and better. I think raw intelligence has become more important than ever, and especially your ability to deeply understand complex subjects and find solutions.

During the last few years, I feel like I've been living on auto mode. Since I've been trying and switching between a lot of different fields, I never actually got to deeply understand things. For example, taking my role as a DS, I have a very practical approach because my initial goal was to be operational in the field and start doing projects. But now that I'm fully 100% involved in the subject, and I know I won't be switching any time soon, I believe it's time that I actually get very good at it and have a very deep understanding of it.

Also, I believe that with AI, we will be having two categories of engineers: the very good ones — that can justify and understand why they chose a certain model, that understand the business needs, can communicate with users/stakeholders, and can ship to production — and then there is the rest.

So, I took the decision to start again from scratch in the field of data science.

## The Plan

1. **Fundamentals** — go over the core math and statistics foundations.
2. **ML Models & Implementations** — study the models and build my own implementations (ThePrimeagen highly recommends this approach).
3. **Python Skills** — focus on craftsmanship and high performance.

This repo will keep track of my advancements. For each subject, there will be notes with my own reflections and implementations.
